Predictive Models & Sources

This simulator implements established sports science models adapted for educational and informational use. The Riegel Power-Law formula is based on Pete Riegel's 1977 empirical research published in American Scientist. The Critical Speed model derives from foundational work by Hill (1925) and subsequent developments by Monod & Scherrer (1965) and Morton (1996). The MSS/MAS Blend formula references methodology described in Loporto & Mannion (2021), Aerobic and Anaerobic Speed Predicts 800-m Running Performance in Young Recreational Runners (PMC8176219). The Speed Reserve Ratio (SRR) framework is based on Sandford et al. (2019), Anaerobic Speed Reserve: A Key Component of Elite Male 800-m Running (PubMed 30300023). Ensemble weighting methodology is adapted from general principles of model averaging in predictive analytics and is not derived from any single proprietary source.

Not Medical or Training Advice

The predictions, fatigue estimates, and pacing recommendations generated by this tool are purely informational and educational. They do not constitute medical advice, clinical exercise guidance, or personalised training prescription. Individual athletic performance is affected by factors this tool does not account for — including injury history, current fitness level, sleep, nutrition, altitude acclimatisation, psychological state, and race-day conditions. Always consult a qualified coach, sports scientist, or licensed physician before making decisions about your training or competition strategy. Do not use this tool to push through pain, illness, or injury.

Accuracy & Limitations

Predicted times are statistical estimates for outdoor track and field with inherent uncertainty. Typical model error for well-trained athletes is approximately ±2–5 seconds for the 800m; errors may be larger for athletes whose profiles fall outside the collegiate-to-elite range on which these models were validated. Sex-adjustment coefficients are population-level averages and may not reflect individual variation. No warranty, express or implied, is made regarding the accuracy of any prediction. Results should not be used as the sole basis for race entry standards, scholarship decisions, medical clearance, or any consequential determination.
